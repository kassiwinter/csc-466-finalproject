NewsWorldAmericasUS politicsJustice Sonia Sotomayor wrote in her dissent that the Supreme Court reached its decision 'by adopting a backward-looking approach'Ruth Bader Ginsburg discusses when there will be 'enough' women on the Supreme CourtRuth Bader Ginsburg has sided with the Supreme Court’s conservative justices in a criminal sentencing case that ended with a 5-4 decision.The court determined a defendant can be sentenced for violating their supervised release — even if that release was to expire during their incarceration while facing new charges.Justice Sonia Sotomayor wrote in her dissent that the Supreme Court reached its decision “by adopting a backward-looking approach.” “Because I cannot agree that a person ‘is imprisoned in connection with a conviction’ before any conviction has occurred, I respectfully dissent,” she added. Ms Ginsburg joined Justices Clarence Thomas, Brett Kavanaugh, John Roberts and Samuel Alito in the majority decision.

The defendant had served 84 months as part of a conviction surrounding felony gun and drug charges, but he faced new additional charges in 2015 and 2016.
